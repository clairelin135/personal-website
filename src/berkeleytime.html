BerkeleyTime Redesign

A personal project to redesign the BerkeleyTime course scheduler commonly used by Cal students

Introduction

BerkeleyTime is a course discovery platform developed by a team of undergraduates; its search capabilities and accessible course insights make it an asset to the university community. As a thought exercise to analyze the role of design in ensuring intuitive website interactions, I challenged myself to streamline its design.

Several screens of the original BerkeleyTime design

User Research

After surveying three students who frequently use BerkeleyTime, I identified key strengths and areas of improvement for the use of the platform. I chose to focus on the two major features of the platform: the course catalog, which students use to browse classes, and the scheduler, which allows students to view different scheduling options for courses.

Strengths
  • Easy way to search, filter, and sort classes
  • Ability to view viable schedules
  • Accessible insights, such as average grade and enrollment status
Areas of Improvement
  • Ease of organizing and scheduling classes in consideration
  • Cumbersome navigation to view class details
  • Inability to locate available classes in a given time frame
Mid-Fidelity Designs
Course Catalog Design Considerations
  • Eliminated cumbersome navigation to view course details by displaying information in hidden dropdowns
  • Delineated a clear user flow by adding a floating action button to add course to scheduler
  • Exposed key course details such as class size, time, and location in immediate course overview
  • Established clear filtering options by converting filter tabs to hidden dropdowns
  • Added key ability to filter for classes in a given time frame
  • Ensured ease of browsing by maintaining dense course toggle list and panel layout

Mid-fidelity redesign of course catalog

Scheduler Design Considerations
  • Reduced multi-step display options to a single-screen overview with schedule management and editing capabilities
  • Increased user flexibility through a dense course selection toggle menu, with details hidden in dropdowns
  • Maintained accessibility for manipulating schedule through simple modals

Mid-fidelity redesign of scheduler

High-Fidelity Designs
Course Catalog Design Considerations
  • Ensured ease of course identification through consistent colors along toggle menu and panel component
  • Reduced convoluted course details by partitioning information into toggling tabs
  • Adjusted time selection filter to allow greater schedule flexibility
  • Restructured minor details such as filter order and course statistics to yield a clear linear reading direction
  • Maintained consistent application branding by sharing same theme color as original site

High-fidelity redesign of course catalog

Scheduler Design Considerations
  • Finalized detailed course options such as editing and deleting courses from scheduler
  • Ensured ease of course identification by implementing consistent colors along toggle menu and calendar component
  • Established clear action hierarchy through primary and secondary buttons on modal
